A BIRD CALL CLASSIFIER PROJECT

A project to study Neural Networks and apply them to classify bird calls

BACKGROUND

The birds in my backyard

Since I moved into my new house in Atlanta, I have been surrounded by beatiful birds and their songs. Since my knowledge about birds is close to zero, I can't identify them

The Idea

Identifying birds is not easy for the untrained eye (or ear). But could we identify them using Machine Learnig?

The Challenge

Select ten bird species common in the Atlanta area and identify them by training a ML model using a collection of sound clips

The Data

From xeno-canto, using its API, one hundred recordings in mp3 format, for each bird were downloaded. This compressed audio format would have to be decompressed to wav for the audio analysis

The Birds

The selected birds for this project are:
American Goldfinch, American Robin, Barn Swallow, Blue Jay, Blue-grey Gnatcatcher, Carolina Chickadee, Carolina Wren, Cedar Waxwing, Northern Cardinal and the Ruby-crowned Kinglet

THE RESULTS

Accuracy

Using the Mel features we get the following:

Testing Accuracy: 0.57277
